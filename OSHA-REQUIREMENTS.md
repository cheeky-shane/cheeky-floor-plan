# OSHA Safety Requirements for Cold Process Soap Manufacturing Facility
## Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania | ~4,700 sq ft Facility

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** January 23, 2026  
**Prepared for:** Cold Process Soap Production Facility

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the comprehensive federal OSHA regulations, Pennsylvania state requirements, and industry best practices applicable to a cold process soap manufacturing facility. Cold process soap making involves handling **sodium hydroxide (lye)** - a highly corrosive substance - along with oils and essential oils (some flammable), requiring robust safety protocols.

### Key Compliance Areas:
1. **Hazard Communication Program** (29 CFR 1910.1200) - MANDATORY
2. **Emergency Eye Wash & Shower Stations** (29 CFR 1910.151) - MANDATORY (due to lye handling)
3. **Fire Protection Equipment** (29 CFR 1910.157) - MANDATORY
4. **Personal Protective Equipment** (29 CFR 1910.132-138) - MANDATORY
5. **Emergency Action Plan** (29 CFR 1910.38) - MANDATORY
6. **Exit Routes** (29 CFR 1910.36-37) - MANDATORY
7. **First Aid** (29 CFR 1910.151) - MANDATORY
8. **Flammable Liquid Storage** (29 CFR 1910.106) - REQUIRED for essential oils

### Critical Requirements Summary:
| Requirement | Specification |
|------------|---------------|
| Eye wash stations | Within 10 seconds (~55 feet) of lye handling |
| Emergency shower | Within 10 seconds of lye handling |
| Fire extinguishers | Every 75 feet (Class A) / 50 feet (Class B) |
| Emergency exits | Minimum 2 exits, located far apart |
| First aid supplies | Readily available, trained personnel |
| Chemical storage | Segregated, proper containment |
| SDS sheets | Accessible within each work shift |

## 1. Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200)

### 1.1 Written Hazard Communication Program
**Required:** Written program maintained at the workplace, available to employees.

**Must Include:**
- [ ] List of all hazardous chemicals present in the workplace
- [ ] Methods for informing employees of hazards of non-routine tasks
- [ ] Methods for informing contractors of hazards
- [ ] Labels and other forms of warning
- [ ] Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
- [ ] Employee training program

### 1.2 Safety Data Sheets (SDS) Requirements
**Required chemicals for soap making requiring SDS:**
| Chemical | Hazard Classification |
|----------|----------------------|
| Sodium Hydroxide (Lye) | Corrosive, Skin/Eye damage Cat. 1 |
| Essential Oils (various) | Flammable Liquid (varies by oil), Skin sensitizer |
| Fragrance Oils | Varies - check individual SDS |
| Carrier Oils | Generally non-hazardous |
| Colorants/Additives | Check individual SDS |

**SDS Management:**
- [ ] Maintain SDS for ALL hazardous chemicals
- [ ] SDS must be readily accessible during each work shift
- [ ] Electronic access permitted if no barriers to immediate access
- [ ] Keep SDS current (request from suppliers)

### 1.3 Container Labeling Requirements
All containers must display:
- [ ] Product identifier (chemical name)
- [ ] Signal word (Danger/Warning)
- [ ] Hazard statement(s)
- [ ] Pictogram(s)
- [ ] Precautionary statement(s)
- [ ] Supplier identification

**Workplace containers** (secondary containers) require:
- [ ] Product identifier
- [ ] Words, pictures, or symbols providing hazard information

### 1.4 Employee Training Requirements
**Initial Training Required For:**
- All employees exposed to hazardous chemicals
- New employees before working with chemicals
- When new hazards are introduced

**Training Must Cover:**
- [ ] Location and availability of the written HazCom program
- [ ] Physical and health hazards of chemicals
- [ ] Protective measures (PPE, work practices)
- [ ] How to read labels and SDS
- [ ] Emergency procedures

**Documentation:**
- [ ] Maintain training records
- [ ] Document date, trainer, and topics covered

**Written Certification Must Include:**

## 3. Emergency Eye Wash & Shower Equipment (29 CFR 1910.151(c))

### 3.1 OSHA Requirement
> "Where the eyes or body of any person may be exposed to injurious corrosive materials, suitable facilities for quick drenching or flushing of the eyes and body shall be provided within the work area for immediate emergency use."

**Sodium hydroxide is a severe corrosive** - Eye wash and emergency shower are MANDATORY.

### 3.2 ANSI Z358.1-2014 Standards (Industry Best Practice)

#### Eye Wash Station Requirements
| Specification | Requirement |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Location** | Within 10 seconds travel time (~55 feet unobstructed) |
| **Water flow** | Minimum 0.4 gallons per minute (GPM) |
| **Duration** | Minimum 15 minutes continuous flow |
| **Height** | Nozzles 33-53 inches from floor |
| **Water temperature** | Tepid: 60-100°F (16-38°C) |
| **Activation** | Single motion, hands-free operation |
| **Signage** | Highly visible sign required |

#### Emergency Shower Requirements
| Specification | Requirement |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Location** | Within 10 seconds travel time of lye handling |
| **Water flow** | Minimum 20 GPM |
| **Duration** | Minimum 15 minutes continuous flow |
| **Height** | Spray pattern 60-96 inches from floor |
| **Spray diameter** | Minimum 20 inches at 60 inches height |
| **Water temperature** | Tepid: 60-100°F (16-38°C) |
| **Activation** | Single motion (pull rod/lever), stays on |

#### Combination Unit Specifications
**Recommended for soap facilities:** Combination eye wash/shower unit

| Typical Dimensions | Measurement |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Height | 84-96 inches |
| Width | 30-36 inches |
| Depth | 24-30 inches |
| Floor drain area | 30 x 30 inches minimum |

### 3.3 Placement Guidelines

**CRITICAL:** Eye wash/shower must be:
- [ ] On the SAME LEVEL as the hazard (no stairs)
- [ ] Within 10 SECONDS walking distance (~55 feet max)
- [ ] Path must be UNOBSTRUCTED and well-lit
- [ ] NOT through doors that could be locked
- [ ] Located in area with NO electrical hazards
- [ ] Accessible from multiple directions if possible

**Recommended Placement for ~4,700 sq ft facility:**
- **Primary station:** Adjacent to lye mixing/handling area
- **Secondary station:** Consider if lye is used in multiple areas >55 ft apart

### 3.4 Maintenance Requirements
| Task | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Activate plumbed units | Weekly (flush 3 minutes) |
| Visual inspection | Weekly |
| Annual inspection | Per manufacturer guidelines |
| Water temperature check | Monthly |
| Document all inspections | Ongoing |

## 4. Fire Extinguisher Requirements (29 CFR 1910.157)

### 4.1 Fire Classification for Soap Facility
| Class | Materials | Present in Facility |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Class A** | Ordinary combustibles (paper, wood, cloth) | Yes - packaging, pallets |
| **Class B** | Flammable liquids (oils, solvents) | Yes - essential oils, carrier oils |
| **Class C** | Electrical equipment | Yes - mixers, heating equipment |

**Recommended:** ABC-rated multi-purpose dry chemical extinguishers

### 4.2 Distribution Requirements

#### Class A Hazards
- **Travel distance:** Maximum 75 feet to nearest extinguisher
- **For 4,700 sq ft:** Minimum 2-3 extinguishers (depending on layout)

#### Class B Hazards (Essential Oil Storage)
- **Travel distance:** Maximum 50 feet from Class B hazard area
- **Size requirement:** Based on hazard level

### 4.3 Extinguisher Sizing for ~4,700 sq ft Facility

| Extinguisher Type | Minimum Size | Quantity Needed | Placement |
|-------------------|--------------|-----------------|-----------|
| ABC Dry Chemical | 10 lb (4A:60B:C) | 3-4 units | Distributed per travel distance |
| Class K (if cooking oils heated) | 6 liter | 1 unit | Near heating area if applicable |

**Calculation for 4,700 sq ft:**
- Maximum 75 ft travel distance for Class A
- Assuming rectangular space ~47 ft x 100 ft
- Minimum 3 extinguishers placed to ensure coverage
- Additional unit near essential oil storage (Class B hazard)

### 4.4 Mounting & Identification Requirements
| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Mounting height** | Handle 3.5-5 feet from floor (≤40 lb units) |
| **Signage** | Visible identification signs |
| **Obstruction** | Must be readily accessible, unobstructed |
| **Cabinet** | Optional; if used, must not impede access |

### 4.5 Inspection & Maintenance Schedule
| Task | Frequency | Documentation |

- [ ] Exits must be located **as far apart as practical**

| **Door width** | Minimum 28 inches; typically 32-36 inches |

| **Path width** | Minimum 28 inches unobstructed |

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Sign text** | "EXIT" in letters ≥6 inches high |
| **Stroke width** | Principal strokes ≥3/4 inch wide |

- [ ] Adequate lighting at all times

## 8. Chemical-Specific Requirements

### 8.1 Sodium Hydroxide (Lye) - CAS 1310-73-2

#### Exposure Limits
| Standard | Limit |
|----------|-------|
| OSHA PEL | 2 mg/m³ (ceiling) |
| NIOSH REL | 2 mg/m³ (ceiling) |
| IDLH | 10 mg/m³ |

#### Storage Requirements
- [ ] Store in cool, dry, well-ventilated area
- [ ] Keep containers tightly closed
- [ ] Store away from acids (violent reaction)
- [ ] Store away from water sources (exothermic reaction)
- [ ] Use corrosion-resistant containers (HDPE, stainless steel)
- [ ] Secondary containment required (110% of largest container)
- [ ] Label clearly: "CORROSIVE - SODIUM HYDROXIDE"

#### Handling Procedures
- [ ] Never add water to lye (always add lye to water slowly)
- [ ] Mix in well-ventilated area
- [ ] Use appropriate PPE (see Section 2.2)
- [ ] Keep emergency eye wash/shower within 10 seconds
- [ ] Never leave lye solution unattended
- [ ] Use only non-reactive containers (no aluminum, zinc, tin)

#### Spill Response Procedures
**Small Spills (<1 gallon):**
1. Alert nearby personnel
2. Don appropriate PPE
3. Contain spill with absorbent (vermiculite, dry sand)
4. Neutralize with weak acid (citric acid, vinegar) - optional
5. Collect and dispose as hazardous waste
6. Wash area with large amounts of water

**Large Spills (>1 gallon):**
1. Evacuate area
2. Contact emergency services if needed
3. Do NOT attempt cleanup without proper training and equipment
4. Prevent entry into drains/waterways

#### Spill Kit Contents for Lye
| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Chemical-resistant gloves (extra pairs) | Hand protection |
| Face shield | Face protection |
| Chemical goggles | Eye protection |
| Chemical-resistant apron | Body protection |
| Absorbent materials (vermiculite/clay) | Spill absorption |
| Plastic scoop/dustpan | Waste collection |
| Sealable containers | Waste storage |
| Citric acid or vinegar | Neutralization (optional) |
| "Caution" signs/barriers | Area isolation |

### 8.2 Essential Oils - Flammable Liquid Storage

#### Flash Point Classifications (Common Essential Oils)
| Essential Oil | Approximate Flash Point | Flammable Category |
|--------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Tea Tree | 131°F (55°C) | Category 3 |

| Eucalyptus | 120°F (49°C) | Category 3 |
| Citrus oils | 113-122°F (45-50°C) | Category 3 |

## 9. Ventilation Requirements

### 9.1 General Ventilation (29 CFR 1910.94)
**For chemical handling areas:**
- Adequate general ventilation to prevent accumulation of vapors
- Lye mixing area: Local exhaust preferred (captures fumes at source)

### 9.2 Recommended Ventilation Specifications
| Area | Ventilation Type | Air Changes/Hour |
|------|-----------------|------------------|
| Lye mixing | Local exhaust hood | 6-10 ACH |
| General production | General dilution | 4-6 ACH |
| Essential oil storage | Mechanical ventilation | 4-6 ACH |
| Curing room | Passive or mechanical | 2-4 ACH |

### 9.3 Local Exhaust for Lye Handling
**Recommended:** Slot hood or canopy hood over lye mixing station
| Specification | Guideline |
|--------------|-----------|
| Capture velocity | 100-150 feet per minute |
| Hood location | Within 12 inches of emission source |
| Exhaust to | Outside, away from air intakes |

## 10. Pennsylvania-Specific Requirements

### 10.1 OSHA Coverage in Pennsylvania
Pennsylvania is **NOT** an OSHA State Plan state for private sector employers. 

**This means:**
- Federal OSHA standards apply directly
- Enforcement by Federal OSHA
- No state-specific occupational safety regulations beyond federal

**PA Department of Labor & Industry** handles:
- Workers' compensation
- Labor relations
- But NOT occupational safety standards for private employers

### 10.2 Pittsburgh Fire Code Requirements
Pittsburgh follows the **International Fire Code (IFC)** as adopted by Pennsylvania.

**Key Local Requirements:**
- Fire department inspection may be required
- Business license/occupancy permit verification
- Fire suppression system requirements (check with local fire marshal)
- Hazardous materials storage reporting thresholds

**Contact:**
- Pittsburgh Bureau of Fire: (412) 255-2860
- For permit and inspection requirements specific to your location

### 10.3 Building Code Compliance
- Pennsylvania uses International Building Code (IBC)
- Occupancy classification for manufacturing: F-1 or F-2
- May affect exit requirements, fire ratings, etc.
- Consult local building inspector for specific requirements

**Key Placement Rules:**
1. **Eye wash/shower:** Within 55 feet (10 seconds) of lye handling - NO OBSTRUCTIONS
2. **Fire extinguishers:** Maximum 75 feet apart (Class A); 50 feet from Class B hazards
3. **Exits:** Opposite ends of facility, doors swing outward
4. **First aid:** Central, visible location
5. **SDS station:** Accessible during all shifts
6. **Flammable cabinet:** Away from exits, heat sources, and ignition sources
