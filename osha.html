‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Sodium hydroxide is a severe corrosive. Eye wash and emergency shower are MANDATORY when handling lye.

The 10-Second Rule

Eye wash/shower must be within 10 seconds walking distance (~55 feet) of lye handling area with NO obstructions in the path.

‚öóÔ∏è Chemical Storage Requirements

Sodium Hydroxide (Lye) Storage

  • Store in cool, dry, well-ventilated area
  • Keep containers tightly closed
  • Store away from acids (violent reaction)
  • Store away from water sources (exothermic reaction)
  • Use corrosion-resistant containers (HDPE, stainless steel)
  • Secondary containment required (110% of largest container)
  • Label clearly: "CORROSIVE - SODIUM HYDROXIDE"
‚ö†Ô∏è Never add water to lye! Always add lye to water slowly. Mix only in well-ventilated areas.

‚úÖ Compliance Checklist

Initial Setup

Hazard Communication

  • Written HazCom program developed
  • Chemical inventory completed
  • SDS collection organized and accessible
  • Container labeling system implemented
  • Employee training conducted and documented

Emergency Equipment

  • Eye wash/shower installed and tested
  • Eye wash within 10 seconds of lye handling
  • Fire extinguishers installed per layout
  • Fire extinguisher inspection tags in place
  • Emergency exits clearly marked
  • Exit signs illuminated
  • Alarm system operational
  • First aid kit(s) stocked and accessible

Chemical Storage

  • Flammable storage cabinet installed
  • Lye stored properly with secondary containment
  • Chemicals segregated by compatibility
  • Spill kits readily available
  • Chemical labels visible and legible

PPE Program

  • Hazard assessment completed and certified
  • PPE selected for all operations
  • PPE inventory stocked
  • PPE training conducted
  • PPE storage area designated
